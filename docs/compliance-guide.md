# NomOS Compliance Guide

## Overview

NomOS enforces a subset of EU AI Act and DSGVO (GDPR) requirements through software controls. This document is honest about what NomOS covers, what it partially covers, and what it does not cover.

---

## EU AI Act Coverage

### Art. 4 — AI Literacy

| Status | **Partially covered** |
|--------|----------------------|
| What NomOS does | Generates compliance documentation (DPIA, transparency declaration, logging policy) that can serve as training material. The `ai_disclosure` field in every manifest provides a standard AI disclosure text. |
| What NomOS does NOT do | Does not provide training programs, e-learning, or literacy assessments. Documentation generation is not the same as organizational AI literacy. |
| What you still need | An organizational AI literacy program. NomOS documents can be inputs to that program. |

### Art. 6 — Risk Classification

| Status | **Covered** |
|--------|-------------|
| What NomOS does | Every agent manifest includes a `risk_class` field (`minimal`, `limited`, `high`). Risk class determines governance requirements — high-risk agents require a kill switch authority. The compliance engine enforces risk-class-specific rules at the gate. |
| Limitation | NomOS accepts the risk class you assign. It does not autonomously assess whether your classification is correct. |

### Art. 9 — Risk Management System

| Status | **Covered** |
|--------|-------------|
| What NomOS does | The blocking compliance gate prevents agent deployment until all required documents exist. The governance hooks (safety-gate, kill-switch, escalation-tracker) form a runtime risk management layer. |
| Limitation | Governance hooks are defined in the manifest but their runtime enforcement depends on the gateway integration (OpenClaw plugin). |

### Art. 11 — Technical Documentation

| Status | **Covered** |
|--------|-------------|
| What NomOS does | `nomos verify` performs a complete technical documentation check: manifest schema validation, business rule validation, compliance document presence, manifest hash integrity, and audit chain integrity. |

### Art. 12 — Record-Keeping

| Status | **Covered** |
|--------|-------------|
| What NomOS does | SHA-256 hash chain audit trail. Every agent lifecycle event is recorded in a tamper-evident JSONL chain. `nomos audit --verify` and the API endpoint `GET /api/audit/verify/{id}` perform cryptographic chain verification. The generated Art. 12 logging policy document describes what is logged and how. |
| What is logged | Agent creation, deployment, compliance checks, governance hook activations, kill switch events, escalations. |

### Art. 13 — Transparency

| Status | **Covered** |
|--------|-------------|
| What NomOS does | Fleet API provides transparent access to agent status, compliance status, and audit trails. The dashboard provides visual oversight of all agents. |

### Art. 14 — Human Oversight

| Status | **Covered** |
|--------|-------------|
| What NomOS does | Kill switch configuration in every manifest. The `kill_switch_authority` field defines who can stop an agent. The generated Art. 14 policy document describes the kill switch mechanism, escalation path, and authorized personnel. High-risk agents are blocked if no kill switch authority is configured. |
| Limitation | Kill switch enforcement depends on the gateway integration. NomOS generates the policy and enforces the configuration requirement, but the actual "stop" mechanism is implemented by the OpenClaw plugin. |

### Art. 26 — Deployer Obligations

| Status | **Covered** |
|--------|-------------|
| What NomOS does | `nomos hire` creates agents with all required metadata. The compliance gate enforces document generation before deployment. The audit trail provides proof of compliance processes. |

### Art. 50 — Transparency Obligations

| Status | **Covered** |
|--------|-------------|
| What NomOS does | Every manifest includes an `ai_disclosure` field (default: "Diese Nachricht wurde mit KI-Unterstuetzung erstellt."). The Art. 50 transparency declaration document is generated by the compliance gate. The `art50-labeler` governance hook (when integrated via gateway) labels AI-generated outputs. |

## DSGVO (GDPR) Coverage

### Art. 28 — Auftragsverarbeitung (Data Processing Agreement / AVV)

| Status | **Not covered** |
|--------|-----------------|
| What NomOS does | Nothing. NomOS does not generate AVV templates. |
| What you still need | A data processing agreement between the AI agent operator and any data processor. This is a legal document that requires legal counsel. |

### Art. 30 — Verarbeitungsverzeichnis (Record of Processing Activities)

| Status | **Covered** |
|--------|-------------|
| What NomOS does | The compliance gate generates a Verarbeitungsverzeichnis document containing: processing purpose, responsible party, contact, data categories, affected persons, recipients, third-country transfers, retention periods, and technical measures. |
| Limitation | The generated document uses data from the manifest. If your manifest data is incomplete or inaccurate, the Verarbeitungsverzeichnis will be too. Review and sign-off by the responsible person is required. |

### Art. 35 — Datenschutz-Folgenabschaetzung (DPIA)

| Status | **Covered** |
|--------|-------------|
| What NomOS does | The compliance gate generates a DPIA document containing: processing purpose, data description, risk assessment, technical and organizational measures, and a conclusion. The risk assessment accounts for PII filter status and memory backend configuration. |
| Limitation | This is a generated template based on manifest data. A real DPIA may require additional analysis specific to your use case. The generated document must be reviewed and signed by the data protection officer. |

### Art. 15 — Auskunftsrecht (Right of Access)

| Status | **Not covered** |
|--------|-----------------|
| What NomOS does | Nothing. NomOS does not provide mechanisms for data subjects to request access to their data. |
| What you still need | A process for handling data subject access requests. If using the Honcho memory backend, this would involve querying Honcho's user data store. |

### Art. 17 — Recht auf Loeschung (Right to Erasure)

| Status | **Not covered** |
|--------|-----------------|
| What NomOS does | Nothing. NomOS does not provide deletion mechanisms for individual data subject records. |
| What you still need | A process for handling erasure requests. If using the Honcho memory backend, this would involve deleting user data from Honcho's store. |

## Generated Compliance Documents

The compliance gate (`nomos gate` / `POST /api/agents/{id}/gate`) generates these 5 documents:

| # | Document | Filename | Legal Basis |
|---|----------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Datenschutz-Folgenabschaetzung (DPIA) | `dpia.md` | Art. 35 DSGVO |
| 2 | Verarbeitungsverzeichnis | `verarbeitungsverzeichnis.md` | Art. 30 DSGVO |
| 3 | Transparenzerklaerung | `art50_transparency.md` | Art. 50 EU AI Act |
| 4 | Human Oversight Policy | `art14_killswitch.md` | Art. 14 EU AI Act |
| 5 | Record-Keeping Policy | `art12_logging.md` | Art. 12 EU AI Act |

All documents are generated in German (as required for DACH-region compliance). They contain data from the agent manifest and must be reviewed and signed by the responsible person before deployment.

---

## Compliance Status Values

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `passed` | All required documents exist, all checks pass |
| `warning` | Documents missing but blocking mode is disabled |
| `blocked` | Documents missing or critical errors — agent cannot deploy |

Blocking mode is enabled by default (`compliance.blocking: true` in manifest). This means an agent cannot be deployed until all 5 documents are generated and present.

## What NomOS Does NOT Cover

To be explicit about the boundaries:

1. **Legal advice** — NomOS generates documents, not legal opinions. All generated documents must be reviewed by qualified personnel.
2. **AVV (Art. 28 DSGVO)** — No data processing agreement templates.
3. **Data subject rights (Art. 15/17 DSGVO)** — No access or deletion mechanisms for individual data subjects.
4. **AI literacy training (Art. 4)** — Documentation only, no training programs.
5. **Autonomous risk classification** — NomOS enforces the risk class you assign but does not assess whether it is correct.
6. **Runtime PII filtering** — PII filter configuration is in the manifest, but actual filtering requires the Honcho memory backend. The local backend does not filter PII.
7. **Conformity assessment** — NomOS does not perform or replace the conformity assessment required for high-risk AI systems under Art. 43 EU AI Act.

## EU AI Act Article 12 — Record-Keeping Event Mapping

Effective 2026-08-02 (Annex III), Article 12 requires high-risk AI systems
to **automatically log events over the system's lifetime**, with a minimum
**six-month retention**. NomOS satisfies this with a HMAC-anchored,
Ed25519-signed, append-only hash chain (`nomos.core.hash_chain`).

The event-type catalog in `nomos.core.events.EventType` is exhaustively
mapped against the three Article 12 logging purposes:

| Art. 12 purpose | NomOS event types written to the chain |
|---|---|
| **(a) Identify situations causing risk or substantial modification** | `compliance.check.failed`, `governance.kill_switch`, `governance.escalation`, `governance.hook.blocked`, `incident.detected`, `incident.escalated`, `tool.call_blocked`, `agent.stale` |
| **(b) Facilitate post-market monitoring** | `agent.deployed`, `agent.retired`, `incident.reported`, `incident.resolved`, `task.failed`, `budget.warning` |
| **(c) Monitor the operation of the high-risk AI system** | `agent.created`, `agent.stopped`, `agent.ended`, `compliance.check.passed`, `compliance.doc.signed`, `governance.hook.triggered`, `tool.call_allowed`, `tool.completed`, `task.created`, `task.assigned`, `task.completed`, `audit.chain.created`, `audit.chain.verified`, `audit.exported` |

### Cryptographic integrity (state-of-the-art per 2026)

Every entry carries:
- **SHA-256 content hash** — entry binds its sequence + timestamp +
  event_type + agent_id + payload + previous_hash. Any byte-level
  change invalidates the recomputed hash.
- **HMAC-SHA256 anchor** — keyed by `NOMOS_HASHCHAIN_HMAC_KEY` (Vault-
  injected, ≥32 bytes). Detects tampering by anyone without the key.
  Fail-closed: missing key or missing `hmac` field on an entry is
  rejected by `verify_chain`.
- **Ed25519 signature** (Phase-A1) — keyed by `NOMOS_AUDIT_SIGNING_KEY`
  (Vault-injected, 32-byte seed). Non-repudiation: a verifier needs
  only the matching public key, no shared secret. Closes the
  retroactive-forgery risk that exists if only the HMAC key leaks.
  Fail-closed: missing key, missing `signature` field, or forged
  signature is rejected.

### Retention (Article 12 minimum 6 months)

Per-agent retention is configured via the manifest field
`manifest.governance.audit_retention_days`. The product enforces a
**hard floor of 180 days** (six months — Art. 12 statutory minimum);
shorter values are rejected at manifest validation. `[SUMMARY]` rows
are always retained beyond the window so the condensed history
survives prune operations.

### Verification by a regulator (no shared-secret needed)

A regulator with only the chain export (`GET /api/agents/{id}/audit/export`)
and the deployment's Ed25519 public key can independently verify:
1. SHA-256 chain consistency,
2. Each entry's Ed25519 signature against the public key,
3. The continuity of `previous_hash` references back to the genesis hash.

The HMAC key remains operator-controlled and need not be shared with
external auditors.
